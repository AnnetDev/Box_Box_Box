Race Terms

  • Box Box Box!

    Team radio code for "COME IN FOR A PIT STOP RIGHT NOW!" Repeated three times because apparently once isn't urgent enough. Mechanics change 4 tyres in 2 seconds while you're still trying to parallel park. Absolute wizardry.

  • Tyres Are Gone

    Lewis Hamilton's favorite phrase. Means the tyres are losing grip and performance. Plot twist: He usually says this and then proceeds to set the fastest lap. It's the F1 equivalent of "I didn't study" and then acing the exam.

  • Undercut

    Pit before your rival, get fresh tyres, go faster, and overtake them while they're still out on old rubber. It's strategic genius when it works, and a disaster when you get stuck in traffic. Chess, but at 300 km/h.

  • Safety Car

    A Mercedes AMG or Aston Martin that comes out when something's gone wrong on track. All cars must slow down and follow it in a sad parade while marshals clean up debris/crashed cars. Ruins your race or saves it—depends on your strategy. Also known as "the great equalizer."

Track elements

  • Hairpin

    A 180° turn where cars slow down from "extremely fast" to "my grandma's Prius." The ultimate test of braking. Also the place where overtakes happen because someone braked 0.2 seconds too late and now they're best friends with the barrier.

  • Chicane

    A sequence of quick left-right-left turns designed to slow cars down and make drivers question their life choices. Basically speed bumps, but make it Formula 1. Watch cars wiggle through like they're dodging responsibility.

  • Straight

    The only part of the track where drivers can actually floor it and reach 350+ km/h. No turning, just pure speed and hoping your engine doesn't explode. Also where DRS turns your car into a rocket ship (terms and conditions apply).

  • Apex

    The geometrically perfect point of a corner. Hit it and you're a hero. Miss it and you're in the gravel, contemplating your choices while marshals wave flags. It's like the sweet spot on a tennis racket, except missing it costs millions.

Tech

  • DRS

    Drag Reduction System

    The rear wing opens up and suddenly you're 15 km/h faster. It's like a cheat code, but legal. Only works if you're within 1 second of the car ahead and in designated zones. Basically: "You're slow? Here, have some extra speed. You're welcome."

  • ERS

    Energy Recovery System

    Harvests energy from braking and exhaust heat to give you an extra 160 horsepower boost. It's a hybrid system that makes your car both fast AND environmentally conscious. Manage it wrong and you're a sitting duck. Manage it right and you're overtaking everyone.

  • KERS

    Kinetic Energy Recovery (retired 2014)

    The OG energy recovery system. Gave you 80 extra horsepower for ~6 seconds per lap. Drivers would save it like a video game power-up, then unleash it for dramatic overtakes. Now replaced by ERS, which is basically KERS but it went to the gym.
